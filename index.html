01 — Where it breaks

Three handovers
Handover 01 — Quote to order

The order does not match the deal

Sales agrees something the order system cannot hold — a price that steps up each year, a discount that stops halfway. Someone re-types it by hand, and their judgement call becomes your contract.

Handover 02 — Order to bill

The customer is billed for the wrong thing

The order says one thing. The billing system reads another. Neither reports an error, because each is doing what it was told. The customer finds it — and only if the bill is too high.

Handover 03 — Bill to the books

The numbers will not survive an audit

Revenue booked against the invoice instead of what you promised to deliver. The auditor asks how you split a bundled deal, and nobody can show the working.

The pattern

No system is at fault

Every platform does exactly what it was set up to do. The money goes missing in the handover between them — and no vendor owns that space.
