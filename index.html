01 — The problem
Most billing problems are not billing problems. The order was taken down wrong months earlier. The invoice is only where it finally shows up.

By the time finance sees the error, the deal is signed, the customer is live, and the invoice has gone out wrong twice. A better billing system will not fix that. The problem sits between the systems you already own.

04 — Where it breaks

Three handovers
Handover 01 — Quote to order

The order does not match the deal

Sales agreed something the order system cannot hold. A price that steps up each year. A discount that stops halfway through. Two contracts lined up to end on the same date. So somebody types it in by hand and makes a judgement call. That judgement call is now your contract. You find out when the customer disputes the first invoice, months later.

Handover 02 — Order to bill

The customer is billed for the wrong thing

The order says the customer is allowed one thing. The billing system thinks they bought another. Their usage gets charged against a plan they never signed. Neither system reports an error, because each is doing exactly what it was told to do. The only person who spots it is the customer, and only if the bill is too high.

Handover 03 — Bill to the books

The numbers will not survive an audit

Revenue gets booked against the invoice instead of against what you actually promised to deliver. When one deal bundles several things together, someone splits the money between them in a spreadsheet. Then the auditor asks how each of those prices was arrived at, and nobody can show the working.

The pattern

No system is at fault

Every platform is doing exactly what it was set up to do. The money goes missing in the handover between them. No vendor owns that space and no dashboard shows it. That is why buying a better billing system rarely fixes it. It is also why this is the whole of our work.
